Getting Started & Login

How do I log in to Webex Connect?

Navigate to your assigned Webex Connect tenant URL (provided by your instructor or shown in the Participant Guide credential bar) and sign in with your seat-specific lab credentials.

Tip: Use an incognito/private browser window to avoid conflicts with any other Webex Connect tenant you may already be logged into.
Do I need anything from Lab 1 before I start?

Yes. This lab assumes the Webex AI Agent from Lab 1 ("Farmers Agent") is already published and reachable. Your instructor will pre-build a renewal-focused AI Agent that all participants reference from their Connect flow — you don't need to build a new agent for Lab 2.

If you skipped Lab 1, you can still complete most of this lab — only the "AI Agent handoff" branch (Module 5) requires the pre-built agent.

Which browser should I use?

Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge (Chromium-based) are recommended. The Webex Connect flow builder is drag-and-drop heavy and works most reliably in those browsers. Safari and Firefox usually work but have occasional UI quirks. Ensure your browser is up to date.

Can I use my personal Webex account for this lab?

No. The lab tenant is pre-configured with the SMS sender, channel assets, and AI Agent integration needed for the lab. Use only the lab credentials assigned to your seat.

Lab Accounts & Seat Numbers

Why do I need a seat number?

All 10 lab participants share the same Webex Connect tenant. Your seat number (1–10) keeps everyone's work separate. It determines:

  • Your login credentials — Each seat has a unique username
  • Your flow name — Renewal Flow N for Seat N
  • Your webhook URL — auto-generated when you save the flow's Inbound Webhook node, unique per flow
Critical: Always name your flow with your seat number. If you use a generic name, another participant may accidentally edit your flow.

Flow Builder Basics

What is a "flow" in Webex Connect?

A flow is a visual diagram that orchestrates a customer interaction. You drag nodes (SMS, Receive, Branch, Delay, etc.) onto a canvas, wire them together with connectors, and Connect executes them in order when the flow is triggered.

For this lab, your flow will: receive a webhook → send an SMS → wait for the customer's reply → branch on what they replied → take a different action per branch.

Where do I find the flow builder?

In Webex Connect, navigate to the left sidebar and select Service Designer (or Flow Designer, depending on tenant version). Choose your assigned service, then click + Create Flow.

Note: Exact menu labels may vary slightly by tenant version. Your instructor will confirm the path during Module 1.
How do I connect two nodes?

Each node has small connection points (usually labeled onSuccess, onTimeout, or branch labels). Click and drag from a connection point on one node to the input of another node to wire them together.

To delete a connector, click it and press Delete (or right-click and choose Remove).

How do I save my work?

Use the Save button in the top-right of the flow canvas. Save frequently — Connect does not auto-save. To make your flow callable, you also need to Publish it (Save creates a draft; Publish makes the active version live).

Webhook Trigger

What is the Inbound Webhook node?

The Inbound Webhook node is the entry point for your flow. When an external system POSTs JSON to this node's URL, the flow starts executing. For this lab, the static "renewal trigger" web page POSTs to your webhook with a phone number; your flow then sends Sarah an SMS.

Where do I find my unique webhook URL?

After you place an Inbound Webhook node and save the flow, click the node and look at its properties panel. You'll see a URL that looks like:

https://api.us.webexconnect.io/resources/v1/events/inbound-webhook/<long-random-id>

The region segment (us, eu, etc.) depends on where your tenant is hosted. Copy the entire URL and paste it into the static renewal-trigger web page.

Important: Each flow has its OWN unique webhook URL. If you create a new flow, you'll get a different URL. Don't share webhook URLs across flows.
What authentication does the webhook need?

For this lab, use the simplest option that the tenant allows — typically Service Key (a header named key with a shared value). Your instructor will share the service key value if needed.

Production deployments should use JWT or HMAC signatures, but those add complexity beyond the scope of a 1-hour lab.

How do I reference the phone number that came in the POST body?

Webex Connect uses dot-notation variables. If the POST body is:

{ "phone_number": "+15551234567" }

You can reference the phone number anywhere downstream as:

$(node.trigger.phone_number) — or $(n1.inbound.phone_number) if your trigger node ID is n1.

Tip: The exact prefix (node.trigger vs nX.inbound) varies by Connect version. The flow builder usually has a variable picker — use it to insert the right syntax automatically.

SMS Send & Receive

How do I send an SMS?

Drag an SMS node onto the canvas. Configure:

  • Destination: the phone number to send to (use your variable, e.g. $(node.trigger.phone_number))
  • From Number: the pre-provisioned SMS sender for the lab tenant (your instructor will share this)
  • Message: the text you want to send (up to 1024 characters)
How do I wait for the customer's reply?

Use the Receive node immediately after your SMS node. Configure:

  • Channel: SMS
  • Max Timeout: 300 seconds (5 minutes) is a good default for the lab

The Receive node automatically correlates the inbound reply to the same flow instance using the customer's phone number. You don't need to manage session IDs manually.

The reply text is captured in a variable like $(nX.receive.message) where nX is the Receive node's ID.

What happens if the customer doesn't reply?

The Receive node has an onTimeout output. Wire that to a fallback action — for this lab, a final SMS like "We didn't hear back. We'll try again later." is a fine default.

My SMS isn't arriving on my phone. Why?

Check, in this order:

  1. Phone number format: must include country code (e.g. +15551234567, not 5551234567)
  2. Country support: the lab tenant's SMS sender may only send to specific countries. Confirm your number's country is supported.
  3. From Number: make sure the SMS node has a valid From Number selected (not blank)
  4. Flow status: the flow must be Published, not just Saved
  5. Webhook 200 OK: the static trigger page should show a 200 response — if it shows 401 or 404, the webhook URL or auth header is wrong

Branching & Logic

How do I branch on the customer's reply?

Drag a Branch node after your Receive node. For each branch, set a condition like:

  • $(nX.receive.message) Equals (ignore case) YES
  • $(nX.receive.message) Equals 1
  • $(nX.receive.message) Equals 2

The Branch node evaluates conditions top-to-bottom; the first match wins. Always include a Default branch for replies that don't match any of your conditions (e.g. "Sorry, didn't understand.").

Should I use "Equals" or "Contains" for matching?

For this lab, Equals (ignore case) is the safest choice — it matches exactly "yes", "YES", "Yes" but rejects "yes please" or "yep". If you want more flexibility, use Contains, but be aware: a reply of "no thanks" would match a "Contains 'thanks'" branch.

Production note: real systems use NLU / intent matching (e.g. via the AI Agent) instead of strict keyword rules. Strict matching keeps the lab simple to demo.

AI Agent Handoff

How does the AI Agent handoff work in this lab?

When Sarah replies 1 (virtual agent), your flow uses the Webex Connect AI Agent node to hand the conversation off to a pre-built Webex AI Agent. The AI Agent then takes over the SMS conversation, answers Sarah's renewal questions, and either completes the renewal or routes back to a human.

Do I need to build the AI Agent myself?

No. Your instructor has pre-built a renewal-focused AI Agent (named something like farmers-renewal-agent) in the same tenant. Your flow just needs to reference it by name in the AI Agent node configuration.

If you completed Lab 1: the agent you built there is a different agent (general policy servicing). The renewal agent is separate. Use the renewal agent for Lab 2.
What should I do if the AI Agent node isn't working?

First, confirm with your instructor that the AI Agent is published and the node configuration matches the tenant. Common issues:

  • Wrong agent name selected in the node properties
  • The pre-built agent isn't in the "Published" state
  • Channel mismatch (the agent must be configured for SMS, not just web chat)
Fallback: if the AI Agent branch isn't working, you can still complete the lab by sending a cosmetic SMS like "A virtual agent will reach out shortly" on that branch. You'll lose the live demo but the flow logic is identical.

End-to-End Testing

How do I test my flow end-to-end?
  1. Save and Publish your flow
  2. Copy your Inbound Webhook URL from the trigger node
  3. Open the renewal-trigger web page (URL provided by your instructor)
  4. Paste your webhook URL into the form
  5. Enter your own mobile number
  6. Click Send Renewal Notice
  7. Watch your phone — you should receive an SMS within ~5 seconds
  8. Reply YES, 1, or 2 and observe the next SMS
How do I see what my flow actually did?

In Webex Connect, navigate to Logs or Flow Executions (label varies by tenant). Find your flow's most recent execution. You can see each node that ran, the variables that were captured, and any errors that occurred.

This is the single most useful tool for debugging. Use it whenever something doesn't behave the way you expect.

Can I test without using my real phone number?

Technically yes — you can put any registered test number — but the experience of seeing a real SMS arrive on your own phone is the most memorable part of the lab. We strongly recommend using your real number.

Your number is used only for the duration of the lab and is not stored anywhere persistent.

Troubleshooting

The static page shows "401 Unauthorized" when I click Send.

The webhook URL is correct but the auth header is missing or wrong. Check:

  • The static page form includes a "Service Key" field — make sure you pasted the value your instructor provided
  • The Inbound Webhook node in your flow has Service Key auth enabled (not "None" — but not JWT either, unless you configured a JWT)
The static page shows "404 Not Found".

The webhook URL is wrong, or the flow isn't published. Re-copy the URL from the trigger node and verify the flow is in the Published state, not Draft.

I got the first SMS, but my reply didn't trigger anything.

Several possible causes:

  • Receive node missing or misconfigured: check that there's a Receive node directly after the SMS node, set to channel SMS
  • Branch conditions don't match: if you replied "yes" but your branch uses "Equals YES" (case sensitive), it won't match. Switch to "Equals (ignore case)"
  • Reply timed out: the Max Timeout was too short and the flow already exited via onTimeout before your reply arrived. Bump it to 300 seconds.
  • Default branch missing: if your reply doesn't match any condition and there's no Default branch, the flow may silently end

Check the flow execution log to see exactly which node fired and what value the Receive node captured.

Two SMS arrive instead of one.

You probably have two SMS nodes wired in series, or you wired both branch outputs to the same SMS node. Check your flow diagram — each path should have exactly the SMS sends you intend.

The flow runs but no execution log appears.

Logs may be delayed by 30–60 seconds in some tenants. Wait, then refresh. If still nothing, the webhook POST may not have actually reached the flow — check the static page's response code and the network tab in your browser's dev tools.

Tenant, Sandbox & SMS Senders

Are we using the production Webex Connect or the sandbox?

This lab uses a dedicated Webex Connect tenant provisioned for the Farmers account team. It is not the public sandbox (sandbox.us.webexconnect.io), and not a customer production tenant.

The tenant has the SMS sender, AI Agent, and channel assets pre-configured for the lab.

What SMS sender (From Number) does the lab use?

A long code or short code is pre-provisioned in the lab tenant. It will appear in the SMS node's "From Number" dropdown — your instructor will confirm which one to select.

Can I send SMS to any country?

No. The lab tenant's SMS sender supports a defined list of countries (typically US/Canada and a few others). If your phone number is outside the supported list, you won't receive the test SMS. Your instructor can confirm the supported countries before the lab starts.

After the lab, can I keep using this Connect tenant?

The lab tenant is shared and may be reset between sessions. For your own experimentation, sign up for a free Webex Connect Sandbox at sandbox.us.webexconnect.io. The sandbox includes most of the same nodes (with a 10K-message lifetime quota) and is free.

General Concepts

What's the difference between Webex Connect and Webex AI Agent Studio?

Webex AI Agent Studio (Lab 1) is where you build the conversational AI itself — knowledge base, intents, prompt behavior, response style.

Webex Connect (Lab 2) is the orchestration layer — it sends and receives messages across channels (SMS, WhatsApp, email, voice), routes them through business logic, and can hand conversations off to an AI Agent or a human agent.

Said another way: AI Agent Studio is the "brain"; Connect is the "nervous system" that delivers messages to and from the brain.

Is this proactive outreach or reactive?

Proactive. Lab 1 was reactive — Sarah initiates the chat by visiting a web page or calling in. Lab 2 is proactive — Farmers initiates by sending Sarah an unsolicited (but expected) renewal reminder. This is one of Connect's key differentiators: it can drive outbound, scheduled, or event-triggered conversations across channels.

What other channels could this flow use besides SMS?

The same flow logic could run over WhatsApp, Apple Messages for Business, Facebook Messenger, RCS, email, or voice. You'd swap the SMS node for the corresponding channel node, and Connect handles the rest. A common production pattern is "channel preference" — try SMS first, fall back to email if no reply in N hours.

Could I add a real human agent handoff?

Yes. Webex Connect integrates with Webex Contact Center. The "human agent" branch in this lab is cosmetic (just sends a confirmation SMS), but in production you'd add a Contact Center node that creates a task and routes it to an available agent. Showing the handoff requires having Contact Center configured, which is outside the scope of a 1-hour lab.
