Webex Connect Lab

Farmers Insurance — Proactive Policy Renewal Outreach

Duration: Approximately 60 minutes | Level: Hands-on | Focus: Webex Connect Flow Builder

Welcome

Welcome to the Farmers Insurance Webex Connect Lab. In this hands-on session, you'll build a proactive SMS flow that reaches out to policyholders 30 days before their renewal, captures their reply, and routes them to the right outcome — all without a single line of code.

By the end of this lab, you will have:

  • Created a Webex Connect flow triggered by a webhook
  • Sent a real SMS to your own phone
  • Captured and branched on the customer's reply
  • Configured per-branch actions (confirmation, AI Agent handoff, live agent escalation)
  • Tested the entire flow end-to-end

Prerequisites:

  • Access to Webex Connect (see Your Lab Credentials below)
  • A mobile phone capable of receiving SMS
  • A modern web browser (Chrome recommended)

Your Lab Credentials

Use Case: "Proactive Policy Renewal"

The Scenario: Sarah Smith's auto insurance policy (FRM-101, covering her 2022 Toyota RAV4) renews in 30 days. Instead of waiting for Sarah to call or visit the website, Farmers Insurance proactively sends her an SMS:

"Hi Sarah, your auto policy (FRM-101) for the 2022 Toyota RAV4 renews in 30 days. Reply YES to confirm renewal, 1 to chat with our virtual agent, or 2 to talk to a person."

Why This Matters to Farmers Insurance:

Business ImpactData Point
Policies that lapse due to missed renewal12% of all policies annually
Cost to re-acquire a lapsed customer$450 average
Customer satisfaction with proactive reminders87% positive sentiment
Renewal completion rate with SMS reminder34% higher than email-only

This isn't just a convenience feature — it's a revenue protection mechanism. Every policy that renews automatically is one less customer shopping competitors.

The Three-Way Reply

Sarah has three options when she receives the SMS:

  1. Reply "YES" — Instant confirmation. Flow sends back: "Great! Your policy is renewed for another year. Confirmation #FRM-101-RENEW."
  2. Reply "1" — Virtual agent handoff. The Webex AI Agent (built in Lab 1) takes over the SMS conversation to answer renewal questions.
  3. Reply "2" — Live agent escalation. Flow asks: "Reply A to be called back, or B to chat by SMS." Then routes accordingly.

If Sarah replies with anything else (or doesn't reply), the flow has a fallback: "Sorry, I didn't understand. Reply YES, 1, or 2."

Key Concepts:

  • Webhook Trigger — External system (or static web page) POSTs JSON to start the flow
  • SMS Node — Sends SMS to a phone number (static or variable)
  • Receive Node — Pauses the flow until an inbound SMS arrives (or timeout)
  • Branch Node — Conditional logic: if reply = "YES", do X; if reply = "1", do Y; etc.
  • AI Agent Node — Hands off the conversation to a Webex AI Agent (from Lab 1)

Module 2: Create Your Flow + Webhook Trigger

Estimated time: 10 minutes

Every flow starts with a trigger. Yours will be an Inbound Webhook.

Step 1: Create Your Flow

1
Create Your Flow
  1. Log in to Webex Connect using your lab credentials
  2. In the left navigation, find and click Service Designer (or Flow Designer, depending on your tenant version)
  3. Select your assigned service (your instructor will confirm the service name)
  4. Click + Create Flow (upper right)
  5. Enter the flow name: Renewal Flow N (where N is your seat number)
  6. Click Create
📌 Use your seat number! Since all 10 participants share the same Webex Connect tenant, you must include your seat number in your flow name. This ensures you can easily find your flow in the shared list and avoid accidentally editing someone else's work.
✓ Checkpoint: You should now see an empty flow canvas with a node palette on the left.

Step 2: Add the Inbound Webhook Node

2
Add the Inbound Webhook Node
  1. In the node palette on the left, find the Inbound Webhook node (it may be under a "Triggers" or "Start" category)
  2. Drag the Inbound Webhook node onto the canvas
  3. Click the node to open its properties panel on the right
  4. Configure authentication:
    • If your instructor provided a Service Key, select Service Key from the dropdown and enter the key value
    • If no key was provided, select None (acceptable for lab purposes)
  5. Click Save in the top-right corner of the flow canvas
Important: The webhook URL only appears AFTER you save the flow for the first time. If you don't see a URL yet, make sure you've clicked Save.

Step 3: Copy Your Webhook URL

3
Copy Your Webhook URL
  1. Click the Inbound Webhook node again to open its properties
  2. You should now see a Webhook URL field that looks like:
    https://api.us.webexconnect.io/resources/v1/events/inbound-webhook/<long-id>
  3. Click the Copy button next to the URL (or select and copy manually)
  4. Paste the URL somewhere you can find it later — a text file, sticky note, or scratch browser tab
This URL is unique to YOUR flow. Don't share it with other participants. Each person's flow has its own webhook URL. If you create a new flow, you'll get a different URL.
✓ Checkpoint: You have a saved flow with an Inbound Webhook node and a copied webhook URL.

Module 3: Send the Renewal SMS

Estimated time: 10 minutes

Now you'll add an SMS node that sends the renewal reminder to Sarah's phone.

Step 1: Add the SMS Node

1
Add the SMS Node
  1. In the node palette, find the SMS node
  2. Drag it onto the canvas, placing it to the right of the Inbound Webhook node
  3. Wire the two nodes together:
    • Click the small connection point on the Inbound Webhook node (usually labeled onSuccess)
    • Drag to the input of the SMS node
What does wiring do? It tells Connect: "When the webhook receives a POST, immediately run the SMS node next." Without the wire, the SMS node would never execute.

Step 2: Configure the SMS Node

2
Configure the SMS Node
  1. Click the SMS node to open its properties panel
  2. Configure the Destination (To) field:
    • Click the variable picker icon (usually a {x} or $ symbol)
    • Navigate to the Inbound Webhook node's output variables
    • Select phone_number (the exact path may be $(node.trigger.phone_number) or $(n1.inbound.phone_number) depending on your tenant version)
Variable syntax note: The exact variable prefix may vary by tenant version. Always use the variable picker to insert the correct syntax automatically — don't type it from memory.
  1. Configure the From Number field:
    • Click the dropdown and select the pre-provisioned SMS sender for the lab
    • Your instructor will confirm which sender to use if multiple options appear
  2. Configure the Message field. Paste the following:
Hi Sarah, your auto policy (FRM-101) for the 2022 Toyota RAV4 renews in 30 days. Reply YES to confirm renewal, 1 to chat with our virtual agent, or 2 to talk to a person.
  1. Click Save in the top-right corner
✓ Checkpoint: Your flow now has two nodes: Inbound Webhook → SMS. When triggered, it will send an SMS to the phone number provided in the webhook POST.

Module 4: Wait for Reply + Branch on Response

Estimated time: 15 minutes

This is the hardest module. You'll add a Receive node (to wait for Sarah's reply) and a Branch node (to route based on what she said).

Step 1: Add the Receive Node

1
Add the Receive Node
  1. In the node palette, find the Receive node
  2. Drag it onto the canvas, placing it to the right of the SMS node
  3. Wire the SMS node's onSuccess output to the Receive node's input
  4. Click the Receive node to open its properties
  5. Configure:
    • Channel: Select SMS
    • Max Timeout: Enter 300 (seconds = 5 minutes)
  6. Click Save
What does the Receive node do? It pauses your flow and waits for an inbound SMS from the same phone number. If Sarah replies within 5 minutes, the flow continues via the onSuccess path. If she doesn't reply, the flow exits via the onTimeout path (which you can wire to a fallback action).
Session correlation: Webex Connect automatically correlates Sarah's reply to the same flow instance using her phone number. You don't need to manage session IDs manually.

Step 2: Add the Branch Node

2
Add the Branch Node
  1. In the node palette, find the Branch node
  2. Drag it onto the canvas, placing it to the right of the Receive node
  3. Wire the Receive node's onSuccess output to the Branch node's input
Why after Receive? The Branch node needs to check the value of Sarah's reply. That value only exists after the Receive node captures it. Order matters: SMS → Receive → Branch.

Step 3: Configure Branch Conditions

3
Configure Branch Conditions

You'll create 3 conditions plus a Default branch.

  1. Click the Branch node to open its properties
  2. Click + Add Condition (or similar button)
  3. Configure Branch 1 (YES):
    • Variable: Use the variable picker to select the Receive node's captured message (usually $(nX.receive.message) where nX is the Receive node's ID)
    • Operator: Select Equals (ignore case)
    • Value: Enter YES
  4. Click + Add Condition again
  5. Configure Branch 2 (Virtual Agent):
    • Variable: Same as above ($(nX.receive.message))
    • Operator: Equals (case-sensitive is fine for "1")
    • Value: Enter 1
  6. Click + Add Condition again
  7. Configure Branch 3 (Live Agent):
    • Variable: Same as above
    • Operator: Equals
    • Value: Enter 2
  8. Ensure there is a Default branch (this should be automatic; if not, add it)
  9. Click Save
Variable syntax reminder: The exact variable name depends on your Receive node's ID. Use the variable picker — don't type it manually. If you see $(nX.receive.message) in the dropdown, that's the one you want.
Always have a Default branch! If Sarah replies with something unexpected (like "maybe" or "help"), and there's no Default branch, the flow will silently end. The Default branch is your safety net.
✓ Checkpoint: Your Branch node now has 4 outgoing paths: YES, 1, 2, and Default. You'll wire each path to a different action in Module 5.

Module 5: Configure Per-Branch Actions

Estimated time: 10 minutes

Now you'll wire each branch to its outcome: confirmation SMS, AI Agent handoff, live agent escalation, or fallback.

Branch 1: YES (Instant Confirmation)

1
YES Branch
  1. Drag a new SMS node onto the canvas
  2. Wire the Branch node's YES output to this SMS node
  3. Click the SMS node and configure:
    • Destination: Same variable as before ($(node.trigger.phone_number))
    • From Number: Same sender as before
    • Message: Paste the following:
Great! Your policy is renewed for another year. Confirmation #FRM-101-RENEW. A copy of your updated policy will arrive by email within 24 hours.
  1. Drag an End node onto the canvas (usually in the "Flow Control" category)
  2. Wire the SMS node's onSuccess output to the End node
  3. Click Save

Branch 2: "1" (AI Agent Handoff)

2
AI Agent Branch
  1. Drag an AI Agent node onto the canvas (if available in your tenant)
  2. Wire the Branch node's 1 output to the AI Agent node
  3. Click the AI Agent node and configure:
    • Agent Name: Select farmers-renewal-agent (or the name your instructor provided)
    • Channel: Ensure SMS is selected (if available)
  4. Wire the AI Agent node's output to an End node
  5. Click Save
AI Agent SMS Caveat: If the AI Agent node isn't supporting SMS in your tenant, your instructor will guide you to a fallback: replace this branch with a cosmetic SMS like "A virtual agent will reach out shortly." Flow logic stays the same; only the live demo magic is reduced. See the FAQ for more details.

Branch 3: "2" (Live Agent Escalation)

3
Live Agent Branch

This branch is a mini-flow: ask the customer how they want to be contacted (call or SMS), then confirm.

  1. Drag a new SMS node onto the canvas
  2. Wire the Branch node's 2 output to this SMS node
  3. Configure the SMS node:
    • Destination: Same variable as before
    • From Number: Same sender
    • Message: Sure! Reply A to be called back, or B to chat by SMS.
  4. Drag a new Receive node onto the canvas
  5. Wire the SMS node's onSuccess to the Receive node
  6. Configure the Receive node:
    • Channel: SMS
    • Max Timeout: 300 seconds
  7. Drag a new Branch node onto the canvas
  8. Wire the Receive node's onSuccess to the Branch node
  9. Configure the Branch node with 2 conditions + Default:
    • Condition 1: $(nY.receive.message) Equals (ignore case) A
    • Condition 2: $(nY.receive.message) Equals (ignore case) B
    • Default: (automatic)

Now wire each of the 3 sub-branches to an SMS node + End node:

Sub-Branch A (Call Back)

  • SMS message: An agent will call you within 15 minutes.
  • Wire to End node

Sub-Branch B (SMS Chat)

  • SMS message: An agent will text you shortly. Look for a message from this number.
  • Wire to End node

Sub-Branch Default (Didn't Understand)

  • SMS message: Didn't catch that. An agent will reach out shortly.
  • Wire to End node
  1. Click Save
Why so many nodes? This branch demonstrates a common pattern: progressive disclosure. Instead of asking "How do you want to be contacted?" in the first SMS (which would be overwhelming), we wait until Sarah chooses "2" (live agent), then ask for her preference. Each step narrows the conversation.

Branch 4: Default (Fallback)

4
Default Branch
  1. Drag a new SMS node onto the canvas
  2. Wire the Branch node's Default output to this SMS node
  3. Configure the SMS node:
    • Destination: Same variable as before
    • From Number: Same sender
    • Message: Sorry, I didn't understand. Reply YES, 1, or 2.
  4. Wire the SMS node's onSuccess to an End node
  5. Click Save
✓ Checkpoint: All 4 branches are now wired. Your flow is functionally complete. Time to publish and test!

Step 5: Publish Your Flow

5
Publish Your Flow

Saving creates a draft. Publishing makes the flow live and callable.

  1. Click Publish in the top-right corner (next to Save)
  2. Confirm the publish action
Draft vs. Published: If your flow is in Draft state, the webhook will return a 404 error when you try to trigger it. Always publish after making changes.

Module 6: End-to-End Test

Estimated time: 5 minutes

This is the moment you've been building toward: send a real SMS to your own phone and watch the flow execute.

Step 1: Trigger Your Flow

1
Trigger Your Flow
  1. Copy your Inbound Webhook URL from Module 2 (if you don't have it handy, click the Inbound Webhook node in your flow and copy it again)
  2. Open the static renewal-trigger web page in a new tab:
    https://farmers-webex-connect-lab-guide.onrender.com/renewal-trigger.html
  3. Paste your webhook URL into the Webhook URL field
  4. Enter your mobile number in the Mobile Number field (include country code, e.g., +15551234567)
  5. If your instructor provided a Service Key, enter it in the Service Key field (otherwise leave blank)
  6. Click Send Renewal Notice
What to expect: Within ~5 seconds, you should receive an SMS on your phone with the renewal message. The static page will show the HTTP response code (200 = success, 401 = auth error, 404 = flow not found).
Troubleshooting: If you don't receive the SMS, check:
  • Phone number format (must include country code, e.g., +1 for US)
  • Flow is Published (not Draft)
  • Static page shows 200 OK response (if 401 or 404, see FAQ)
  • Your country is supported by the lab tenant's SMS sender (ask your instructor)

Step 2: Test Each Branch

2
Test Each Branch

Once you receive the first SMS, reply to test each branch:

  1. Reply "YES" — You should receive the confirmation SMS within ~3 seconds
  2. Re-trigger the flow from the static page (paste webhook URL + your number again)
  3. Reply "1" — If the AI Agent node is working, you'll be handed off to the virtual agent. If not, you'll receive the fallback SMS.
  4. Re-trigger the flow again
  5. Reply "2" — You should receive the follow-up SMS asking "A or B". Reply with either letter and observe the confirmation.
  6. (Optional) Re-trigger and reply with something random like "maybe" — you should receive the Default branch fallback SMS.
What if my reply doesn't trigger anything? Check the flow execution logs in Webex Connect (usually under "Logs" or "Flow Executions" in the left nav). You'll see exactly which nodes ran, what variables were captured, and where the flow stopped. Common issues:
  • Receive node timed out before you replied (bump timeout to 300 seconds)
  • Branch condition is case-sensitive and you replied "yes" instead of "YES" (use "Equals (ignore case)")
  • No Default branch, so unexpected replies silently end the flow
🎉 Congratulations! You've built a working proactive SMS flow with branching, AI Agent handoff, and live agent escalation — all in under 60 minutes.

What You Built

In this lab, you created a production-ready pattern for proactive customer outreach:

  • Webhook-triggered flow — Any system that can POST JSON can start this flow (CRM, renewal management system, scheduled job)
  • Two-way SMS conversation — Not just broadcast; you captured Sarah's reply and routed based on her intent
  • Conditional branching — Different outcomes for different replies, with a safety-net Default branch
  • AI Agent handoff — Seamless transition from flow orchestration to conversational AI
  • Progressive disclosure — Asking follow-up questions only when needed (e.g., "A or B" only after Sarah chose "2")

Why this matters: You didn't write a single line of code. No SMS gateway integration, no session management, no state machines. Webex Connect abstracted all of that. What would have taken weeks of engineering work, you built in an hour.

Next Steps

Here are some ways to extend what you built:

  1. Swap SMS for WhatsApp — Change the SMS nodes to WhatsApp nodes. Same logic, different channel. Webex Connect supports WhatsApp, Apple Messages for Business, Facebook Messenger, and more.
  2. Add scheduled triggers — Instead of manually triggering from a web page, configure a scheduled job that fires the webhook 30 days before each policy's renewal date (pulled from a CRM or policy management system).
  3. A/B test message variants — Use Connect's experiment nodes to test different message wording and see which gets higher reply rates.
  4. Real human agent handoff — Integrate with Webex Contact Center to route the "2" branch to a live agent queue instead of just sending a confirmation SMS.
  5. Add delay nodes — If Sarah doesn't reply within 5 minutes, wait 24 hours and send a follow-up SMS: "Just checking in — your policy renews in 29 days. Reply YES to confirm."
